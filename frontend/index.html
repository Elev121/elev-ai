About ELEV

What Is the ELEV Framework?

ELEV (Evaluative Learning & Evidence Validator) is a multi-module AI system designed to address the growing challenge of research quality assurance in academic institutions.

Multi-Dimensional Analysis

Evaluates research across five core dimensions: semantic coherence, citation quality, methodology rigor, originality index, and results clarity.

Real-Time Processing

Delivers comprehensive validation reports in under three minutes — compared to the 3–7 days required for traditional manual review.

Reproducible Results

Algorithmic consistency ensures that identical submissions receive identical scores, eliminating reviewer bias that plagues manual processes.

Institutional Scale

Supports bulk processing for universities and research bodies, with a dashboard for tracking departmental submission trends and quality benchmarks.

Methodology

How ELEV Evaluates Research

A five-stage pipeline combines natural language processing, graph-based citation analysis, and ensemble machine learning to produce a reliable ELEV score.

01

Document Ingestion & Parsing

Research papers (PDF, DOCX, or plain text) are ingested and parsed into structured sections: abstract, introduction, methodology, results, discussion, and references. OCR handles scanned documents.

PDF Parsing OCR Section Detection
02

NLP Feature Extraction

Transformer-based models (BERT, SciBERT) extract semantic embeddings, key entities, and discourse markers. Sentence-level coherence is scored using cosine similarity between adjacent embeddings.

BERT / SciBERT Embeddings NER Coherence Scoring
03

Semantic Citation Discovery

A graph neural network maps the paper's claims to a citation network of 200M+ scholarly works (via Semantic Scholar & OpenAlex APIs). Each citation is validated for relevance, recency, and credibility.

Graph Neural Network Semantic Scholar OpenAlex
04

Integrity & Originality Analysis

Cross-referenced against 150M+ documents using MinHash LSH for near-duplicate detection. Paraphrase detection models identify academic misconduct beyond direct copying.

MinHash LSH Paraphrase Detection Similarity Index
05

Score Aggregation & Report Generation

An ensemble model weights the five dimension scores into a final ELEV Score (0–100), and generates a structured validation report with actionable recommendations and grade classification (A–F).

Ensemble Model Weighted Scoring PDF Report
